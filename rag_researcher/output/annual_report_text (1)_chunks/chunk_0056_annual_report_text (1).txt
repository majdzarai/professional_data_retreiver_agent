EARLY DETECTION SUBSTANTIALLY 
INCREASES THE CHANCES OF SURVIVAL 
3 million undiagnosed cases of childhood cancer. A modeling study published in The Lancet Oncology1 
projected cancer incidence for 200 countries 
worldwide and suggested that the number 
of undiagnosed cases of childhood cancer could 
account for more than half of the total in Africa, south-central Asia and the islands of the Pacific. In North America and Europe, by contrast, only 3% 
of cases are undiagnosed.
